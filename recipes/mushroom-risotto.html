Mushroom Risotto

This creamy and vegetarian bowl of happiness is fully loaded with garlicky mushrooms, topped with melting cheese and easy to make
This is a risotto so good, it will make a grown man weep

PRO TIP - Making risotto can seem like a daunting task but with this smart hack using tinned cream of mushroom soup to create a creamy texture means you get all the flavour with none of the fuss.
Trust us – you won’t go back to making it any other way!

Total time: 45 mins | Easy | serves 4

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 pack (300 g) white mushroom
  • 1 brown onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 300 g risotto rice
  • 1 tin (400 g) cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 vegetable stock pot
  • 2 tbsp parsley, finely chopped
  • 75 g medium cheddar, finely grated

Steps

  1. Heat the oil (1 tbsp) in a large saucepan and cook the mushrooms (1 pack) over a high heat for 4-5 minutes until golden. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  2. Turn the heat down to medium-low, add the onion (1) and cook for 8-10 minutes, until soft but not coloured. Season, add the garlic (2 cloves) and cook for another minute, stirring.
  3. Stir in the rice (300 g) and cook for 1 minute until coated. Add the soup (1 tin) and bring to a gentle bubble before cooking, stirring frequently for 3-4 minutes, until a spoon leaves a trail on the bottom of the pan.
  4. Measure out 1 litre of just-boiled water in a jug and add the stock pot (1) to dissolve. Use a little to swirl out the soup can, then pour the stock into the rice little by little, stirring between each addition, until the rice is creamy but still with a little bite. This will take about 20-25 minutes.
  5. Stir in the mushrooms and most of the cheese (75 g) and parsley (2 tbsp), cooking for 1 minute until the cheese has melted.
  6. To serve, scatter with the remaining parsley and cheese.

Enjoy!
